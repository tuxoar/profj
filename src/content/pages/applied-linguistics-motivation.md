## Research focus

I work in applied linguistics on the development of language-learning motivation,
with a particular interest in what makes motivation *sustainable*. Rather than
treating motivation as a fixed trait, I study it as something that is built and
rebuilt over time — shaped by a learner's **vision** of a future self who uses
the language, by **socialization** into communities where the language matters,
and by a sense of **progression** that carries a learner from the classroom into
real domains of use.

A recurring theme in my work is helping learners **perform beyond proficiency**:
establishing domain expertise so that Chinese becomes a tool for doing meaningful
things, not only a subject to be studied. This bridges applied-linguistics theory
and the day-to-day design of teaching — the thread that connects this work to my
[language pedagogy](/language-pedagogy/).
